<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Mastering Beekeeping and Golden Honey">

The beekeeping system is the mechanical heart of **Disney Dreamlight Valley Honeyglow Woods chapter 2**. To progress the story, you must restore the Busy Bees' Houses. These structures are not just decorative; they are functional resource generators that produce Golden Honey, the primary currency for expansion-specific crafting and quest progression.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Restore the Busy Bees' House">
    Locate the broken apiary in Drowsybloom Acre. You will need 10 Hardwood and 2 Iron Ingots to repair the first unit. Once repaired, the house becomes active.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Strategic Flower Placement">
    Bees require pollination to produce honey. Pick up flowers from around the Woods and replant them within a 5x5 grid of the Busy Bees' House. The rarity of the flower influences the production speed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Harvesting Golden Honey">
    Check the house every 20 minutes. A "sparkling" effect indicates that Golden Honey is ready for collection. This resource is distinct from the "Honey" found in the base game.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Boosting Growth">
    As you level up your beekeeping skills, the bees will provide a "Pollination Aura" that increases the growth speed of nearby crops by 25%.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Success">
You can move Busy Bees' Houses to your main Valley biomes once you reach Friendship Level 10 with Winnie the Pooh, allowing for Golden Honey production outside of the expansion areas.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Exploration Guide: Gloommeadow and Braveheart Grove">

Chapter 2 introduces two distinct biomes that offer unique challenges. The Gloommeadow is a swampy terrain where visibility is low, while the Braveheart Grove is a dense forest filled with ancient trees and hidden secrets. Navigating these areas is essential for solving the mystery of the Everoak Tree.

| Biome Name | Key Landmark | Unique Critter | Environmental Hazard |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Gloommeadow** | Pooh Sticks Bridge | Purple Hedgehog | Thick Fog (Slows movement) |
| **Braveheart Grove** | The Ancient Hollow | Golden Hedgehog | Thorny Brambles (Blocks path) |
| **Nectar Apiary** | Giant Honeycomb | Honey Bee (Companion) | Sticky Ground (Prevents sprinting) |

In the Gloommeadow, you will find the bridge used for the **Pooh Sticks** mini-game. This activity involves four villagers dropping sticks into the river; the first stick to pass under the bridge wins. Playing this daily is the fastest way to increase friendship levels with Eeyore and Piglet during Chapter 2.

<Callout type="info" title="Note">
The "Rainy Day Winnie the Pooh" Dream Style is particularly useful in the Gloommeadow, as it prevents the "Drenched" debuff that occasionally occurs during the marsh's frequent rainstorms.
</Callout>
