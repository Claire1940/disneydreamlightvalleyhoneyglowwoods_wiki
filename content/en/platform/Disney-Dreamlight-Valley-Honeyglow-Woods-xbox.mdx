<Section icon={Target} title="How to Start Your Honeyglow Woods Adventure">

Starting your journey into the Honeyglow Woods is a multi-step process that begins right in your main Valley. After purchasing the DLC, the game triggers a specific questline involving a mysterious delivery. Unlike previous expansions that used the Stardust Port, this adventure is accessed through a magical gateway that you can place anywhere in your world.

The narrative hook involves a strange fog that has descended upon a forgotten corner of the forest, separating the residents of the Hundred Acre Wood. Your first task is to restore the "Tree Sprout" found in your mailbox, which serves as the literal and metaphorical root of the new content.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Claim the Mailbox Package">
    Check your in-game mailbox to find a package containing childhood keepsakes and a magical Tree Sprout. This item is essential for opening the portal.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Plant the Tree Sprout">
    Select a location in your Valley or on the base game Floating Islands to plant the sprout. Once planted, it will instantly grow into a glowing tree gateway.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Enter the Magical Gateway">
    Walk through the portal to transition into the Honeyglow Woods. You will immediately encounter Winnie the Pooh, who provides your first set of objectives.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Clear the Initial Fog">
    Use your Royal Tools to clear the thick fog surrounding the entrance area. This will reveal the path to Drowsybloom Acre and the Everoak Tree.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Portal Placement">
You can move the magical tree gateway using the Furniture Edit mode at any time. Many players prefer placing it near the Dreamlight Library or in the Glade of Trust to match the forest aesthetic.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Beekeeping and Golden Honey Mechanics">

The standout feature of this Adventure Pack is the Beekeeping system. This is a new production loop that allows players to generate **Golden Honey**, a premium ingredient used in high-energy recipes and special furniture crafting. Success in beekeeping requires careful placement of "Busy Bees’ Houses" and strategic floral arrangements.

The system is synergistic; bees require flowers to produce honey, and in return, the presence of a Busy Bees’ House increases the growth rate and spawn frequency of nearby flowers. This makes it an essential mechanic for players who enjoy gardening and landscape design.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Obtain Busy Bees’ Houses">
    Progress through Pooh's early quests to receive your first set of bee houses. Additional houses can be crafted at any workbench.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Strategic Flower Placement">
    Place at least 5-8 flowers within a 3x3 radius of the bee house. The variety of flowers does not affect the honey type but influences the speed of production.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Harvest Golden Honey">
    Wait for the honey meter to fill. Once the house glows with a golden aura, you can harvest the Golden Honey.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Expansion to Other Biomes">
    Once unlocked, Busy Bees’ Houses can be moved to the Plaza, Meadow, or any other base game biome to enhance your local flower gardens.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Mini-Games and Side Activities">

Beyond the main quest, Honeyglow Woods offers relaxed activities designed for "cozy" playstyles. The most prominent is **Pooh Sticks**, a mini-game played at the bridge in the Gloommeadow. You can invite up to three other villagers to participate in a race where you drop sticks into the river and see whose object reaches the finish line first.

Additionally, the expansion introduces **Hedgehogs** as a new critter family. These colorful creatures are found throughout the woods and have unique "Photo Mode" poses. Taming them requires feeding them their favorite snacks found in the Nectar Apiary.
