<Section icon={Shield} title="Beekeeping and Golden Honey Mechanics">

The most significant gameplay addition in the **Disney Dreamlight Valley Honeyglow Woods switch** expansion is the Beekeeping system. This is a functional production loop that allows players to farm a new resource called **Golden Honey**. Unlike regular foraging, beekeeping requires tactical placement of furniture and plants to maximize efficiency.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Obtain Busy Bees’ House">
    Complete the initial quests with Winnie the Pooh to unlock the recipe for the Busy Bees’ House.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Strategic Placement">
    Place the House in a biome. While they work best in Honeyglow Woods, you can also place them in the main Valley.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Flower Pollination">
    Plant or drop flowers within a 5x5 grid of the House. The variety and number of flowers determine the speed of honey production.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Harvest Golden Honey">
    Check the House periodically. When the golden glow is visible, you can harvest Golden Honey for use in cooking and crafting.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Efficiency Warning">
Bees will not produce honey if there are no flowers nearby. Furthermore, a fully pollinated Busy Bees’ House will actually increase the growth speed of the flowers surrounding it, creating a beneficial loop for flower farmers.
</Callout>
